Dear Mr. Buildreps,

Thank you for the careful and detailed commentary. I have read it closely and want to respond to several specific points. The final report has now been updated with the incorporations described below (commit pushed 23 May 2026).

**What is being incorporated.** Your commentary identifies several factual clarifications about the framework that I did not have during the analysis period, and one methodological concession about my characterisation. I have incorporated these in the final report:

1. **The 47°W meridian's derivation history.** Your account of the 2015 hemispheric-intersection calculation (yielding 71.6°N, 47.1°W) and the 2020 recalculation (yielding 58°N, 44°W) provides important context that was not available to me when the pre-registration was drafted. The final report reflects this in §2.7 (look-elsewhere control), with a clear statement that the meridian was, according to your account, theoretically derived rather than selected by post-hoc scanning. The look-elsewhere scan remains a valid descriptive instrument regardless, and the empirical attractor at −20°E remains a descriptive finding that I do not retract.

2. **The Pole II–V derivation as cross-validation rather than independent prediction.** Your clarification that Poles II–V were identified partly through examination of the orientation data is significant context. The final report reflects this, particularly in framing the per-pole tests as cross-validation exercises rather than tests of fully independent predictions.

3. **The block-conditional null's structural limitation.** I accept your criticism that the null's behaviour with respect to cross-regional signals should be made more explicit. §4.5 of the final report is expanded to discuss what the block-conditional null can and cannot detect. I would note, however, that your stronger formulation — that the null is "structurally incapable" of detecting a global cross-cultural signal — is not, in my reading, quite accurate. The null preserves block membership (each structure remains in its original block) and shuffles bearings only within blocks. A cross-regional signal that appears across multiple blocks survives this procedure precisely because the original block-to-block structure is preserved. What the null *does* erase is the possibility that the signal is driven by region-specific bearing patterns alone, and this is the structural limitation worth disclosing. The final report states both — what the null detects and what it absorbs — without endorsing either reading as definitive.

4. **Your alternative interpretive frame for Pole I.** The observation that a "background-consistent" finding for the current pole is not necessarily evidence against your framework — under a model of discrete pole shifts, structures built in the current rotational regime should match the geometric background while older structures should appear as anomalies — is a reasonable reading of the null result. This is noted in §4.6 as a footnote, presented as your alternative interpretation rather than my endorsement.

5. **Your full commentary as Appendix A.** Per the pre-registration's commitment to transparency, your commentary is appended verbatim to the final report as Appendix A. The acknowledgments note that the commentary is included with your permission. Anyone reading the report will have direct access to your perspective alongside the analytical conclusions.

**What is not changed.** Several elements of the report remain as drafted:

6. **The core analytical findings, p-values, and statistical conclusions.** Your commentary does not identify factual errors in the analysis itself. The statistical findings — robust clustering at Poles II and III, null findings for Poles I, IV, V under principled nulls, the §11(b) assignment match at 46% vs 9% expected — stand as they are.

7. **The framing of the abstract and conclusion.** The abstract already leads with the Poles II and III findings, which you asked it to do. The conclusion in §4.7 says "the framework receives partial empirical support" — this is fair to the data. Strengthening it further (e.g., to "the framework is supported") would overclaim relative to what the analysis establishes; you correctly note that the broader interpretive claim is not tested by an orientation-clustering analysis alone, and the conclusion reflects this.

8. **The descriptive longitude scan finding (47°W is rank 10, attractor at −20°E).** This is a falsifiable observation about the data that is independent of any null model. I accept your context about the 2015 derivation, but the descriptive observation about where the observed data is most-tightly clustered remains in the report, alongside your explanation that this is a different metric from the theoretical derivation you used.

9. **The diagnostic narrative in §3.2 and the methodological lesson in §4.4.** These describe the discovery that the pre-registered null was confounded by hemisphere-selection. The methodology section is the section you most explicitly endorsed in your commentary; I will not soften this critique of the unconditional null even though it produced a result formally favourable to your framework.
